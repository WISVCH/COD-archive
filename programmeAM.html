11:45 to 12:30 Academia presentation by Mark Veraar

Mark Veraar is professor at Delft University of Technology. Author of over 60 research papers, and several books. He is a leading researcher in the theory of evolution equations and stochastic partial differential equations. After finishing his PhD in Delft, he held post-doctoral positions at the Universities of Warsaw and Karlsruhe, the latter with a Alexander von Humboldt Fellowship. In 2009 he continued at Delft University of Technology. There he received VENI and VIDI grants from the Netherlands Organisation for Scientific Research.

He has been active in several national committees and boards such as Koninklijk Wiskundig Genootschap, Platform Wiskunde Nederland, and Stieltjes. He is editor for several international journals including Stochastics and Partial Differential Equations: Analysis and Computations, and the Dutch journal Indagationes Mathematicae.

He has taught many bachelor and masters courses in the Applied Mathematics education. Among those are Mathematical Structures and Real analysis, which are two of the basic introductions to mathematical analysis. In 2017 he became teacher of the year for Applied Mathematics and for the Faculty EEMCS.
